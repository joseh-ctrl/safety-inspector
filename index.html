📋 Safety Walkthrough — What We Inspect Biweekly inspection reference for managers
This guide outlines every area reviewed during our biweekly safety walkthroughs. Managers should ensure their departments are compliant before each inspection date.
🏗️ General Safety
🚪
Loading Dock & Fall Prevention
Exposed or open loading dock doors and areas where employees could fall 4 feet or more must be chained off, roped off, or otherwise blocked at all times.
🧹
Floors & Aisles
All floors and aisles must be clear of clutter, electrical cords, hoses, spills and any other hazards that could cause employees to slip, trip or fall. Aisles must remain passable at all times.
💡
Ventilation & Lighting
The department must be well ventilated and well lit. Burned-out lights or blocked vents should be reported and addressed promptly.
🔒
Lockout / Tagout (LOTO) Procedures
Lockout/Tagout procedures must be displayed and posted clearly near the appropriate equipment. All employees must be trained on LOTO before operating machinery.
🦺
Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
Required PPE must be present, accessible and in good condition at all equipment stations. Employees must be using appropriate PPE for their tasks.
📋
Maintenance & Inspection Checklists
Machine Maintenance, 5S, Eyewash Station, First Aid Kit, and Forklift Inspection Checklists must all be completed, current, and available for review.
🟫
Floor Mats (Tape Mats)
All ergonomic and anti-fatigue mats must be properly taped down on all edges to prevent lifting, curling, or creating a tripping hazard. Worn or damaged mats must be replaced immediately.
🧯
Exits, Fire Extinguishers & Electrical Panels
All exits must be unobstructed and clearly marked. Fire extinguisher inspections must be current with a minimum 3-foot clearance maintained. Electrical panels must be free of clutter and fully accessible.
🪨
Wall & Rack Safety
No heavy items such as fabric rolls or materials may lean against walls. All racks must be properly secured and loaded within rated capacity.
📦 Materials Handling Safety
🚜
Aisle & Passageway Clearances
Aisles and passageways where forklifts or other mechanical handling equipment operate must be appropriately marked and maintain safe, unobstructed clearances at all times.
📦
Inventory Storage & Stacking
All inventory must be stored or stacked properly. Any pallet not on the ground must be shrink-wrapped. Materials must be limited in height and stacked to prevent tipping, falling, rolling or spreading.
🗑️
Storage Area Housekeeping
Storage areas must be kept free from accumulation of materials that could lead to tripping hazards, fire risks, chemical reactions or pest infestations.
⚗️ Hazard Communication Safety
🏷️
Chemical Container Labeling
All hazardous materials containers must be properly labeled with the chemical identity, manufacturer name and address, and appropriate hazard warnings. No unlabeled containers are permitted.
📗
Safety Data Sheets (SDS / MSDS)
The MSDS/SDS binder must be kept in an accessible, clearly marked location and must be up to date. All employees must know where to find it.
🧤
PPE When Handling Chemicals
Employees must use proper PPE (gloves, goggles, aprons) whenever handling chemicals. PPE requirements for each chemical are listed in the SDS.
🏪
Chemical Storage Compliance
All chemicals must be stored according to manufacturer recommendations and local/national fire codes. Incompatible chemicals must be stored separately. Secondary containment must be in place for liquid chemicals.
✅ Common Violations & How to Avoid Them
❌ Blocking fire extinguisher
✅ Maintain 3-foot clearance at all times. Use floor markings to designate the zone.
❌ Tape mat edges lifting
✅ Inspect mats weekly. Re-tape or replace immediately when edges lift.
❌ Unlabeled chemical containers
✅ Label all containers before use. Never store chemicals in unmarked containers.
❌ Extension cords in walkways
✅ Route cords along walls or overhead. Request permanent electrical if needed.
❌ Pallets blocking aisles
✅ Stage pallets only in designated areas. Never block marked travel paths.
❌ Checklists not completed
✅ Assign a daily responsible person per checklist. Keep binders updated.

Inspection Frequency
How our safety program works
🔁 Every 2 Weeks
Full safety walkthrough of all departments. Issues are logged with photos and assigned a 2-week deadline.
📸 Photo Required
All issues must be photographed when logged. Managers must upload proof of resolution before marking complete.
📊 Tracked & Reported
All completions and failures are permanently tracked. Monthly and yearly rates are reported to HR and leadership.
